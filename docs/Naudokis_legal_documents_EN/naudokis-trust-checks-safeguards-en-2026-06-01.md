# Naudokis Trust, Checks, and Safeguards Standard

Effective date: June 1, 2026
Version: 2026-06-01
Document ID: **NAUDOKIS-TRUST-SAFETY-EN-2026-06-01**

This Standard explains the practical safeguards that Naudokis applies so that item rental through the Platform is clearer, safer, and more trustworthy. It applies together with the Terms of Use, Payments Rules, Deposit and Damage Rules, Moderation Rules, and Privacy Policy.

## 1. What "protection" means on the Naudokis Platform

Naudokis protection means the combined set of technical, payment, identity, evidence, moderation, and support measures. It helps reduce risk, but does not replace the duties of the Lessor and the Renter.

Naudokis protection includes:

- confirmation of the account, email, telephone, identity and, where applicable, business information;
- payments, Deposits, payout waiting periods, and refunds through the Platform payment process;
- review of Listings, categories, prohibited items, and higher-risk cases;
- submission through the Platform of evidence concerning condition, handover, return, damage, and delay;
- review, rating, complaint, and moderation processes;
- review of fraud, abuse, payment risk, and security signals;
- internal support and Dispute handling.

## 2. What this protection does not mean

Naudokis safeguards are not:

- insurance;
- a guarantee that an item will never be damaged, lost, or returned late;
- a promise to compensate the Lessor's or Renter's losses in all cases;
- a limit on the Renter's liability;
- a substitute for the Lessor's duty to provide a safe and lawful item;
- a substitute for the Renter's duty to use the item with care and return it on time;
- arbitration or a process replacing a court decision.

If a separate insurance, compensation, or guarantee service is clearly indicated in the App for a specific Reservation, the terms of that service apply to it. Until such a separate term is clearly provided, the Platform risk-management model described in this Standard applies.

## 3. Identity and account confirmation

Naudokis may require confirmation of an email address, telephone number, identity, business profile, right of representation, payment instrument, Stripe Connect account, or other data where this is necessary for a specific function.

Confirmation may be mandatory before:

- listing items;
- accepting paid Reservations;
- receiving payouts;
- reserving a higher-risk or higher-value item;
- resolving a payment, Deposit, damage, loss, fraud, or security issue;
- restoring access to an account;
- using business functions.

Confirmed status does not mean that Naudokis guarantees the person's honesty, the quality of the item, or the outcome of the transaction. It means that Naudokis has an additional signal that helps manage risk.

## 4. Risk signals

Naudokis may review a Reservation, Listing, account, payment, or payout if one or more risk signals are detected, for example:

- inconsistencies in identity, telephone, email, payment, or business data;
- an unusually high item value, Deposit, number of Reservations, or payment amount;
- an attempt to bypass the Platform payment or messaging process;
- inconsistencies in Listings, photographs, or descriptions;
- repeated cancellations, no-shows, damage Disputes, or delays;
- payment Disputes, chargebacks, unauthorized cards, or signs of fraud;
- reports of a dangerous, illegal, stolen, or unsafe item;
- use of multiple accounts to bypass restrictions.

## 5. Possible safeguards

Taking the risk into account, Naudokis may proportionately apply one or more measures:

- request additional information or evidence;
- require identity or business confirmation;
- temporarily hold a payout;
- temporarily hold part or all of a Deposit while an asserted claim is being reviewed;
- prevent confirmation of a Reservation;
- hide or remove a Listing;
- restrict a specific category or item;
- suspend an account or certain account functions;
- transfer information to Stripe, an identity verification provider, law enforcement, consumer rights, data protection, tax, or market surveillance authorities where necessary and permitted.

## 6. Evidence standard

In Disputes, the most important evidence is timely, clear, and connected to the specific Reservation. Naudokis assesses:

- Reservation data in the App;
- handover and return times;
- photographs or videos of the item before handover and after return;
- message history on the Platform;
- payment, Deposit, and payout status;
- instructions and defects described by the Lessor;
- the Renter's notices about defects at the time of receipt;
- repair, replacement, cleaning, or other reasonable estimates;
- third-party information where it is necessary and lawfully obtained.

Evidence submitted outside the Platform may be assessed, but evidence submitted in the App on time is generally more reliable because it is easier to link to a specific Reservation, time, and party.

## 7. Higher-risk items

For higher-risk items, Naudokis may apply additional conditions, for example a higher Deposit, identity verification, administrator review, mandatory instructions, an age restriction, an accessories list, handover photographs, or limited availability.

Higher-risk status does not mean that the item is prohibited. It means that clearer conditions and greater care are required.

## 8. Users' duty to cooperate

The Lessor and the Renter must cooperate in good faith where it is necessary to verify identity, payment, Deposit, item condition, damage, delay, cancellation, return, a dangerous item, or another issue. Failure to cooperate may affect the outcome of a Dispute, the refund of a Deposit, a payout, a review, account status, or access to Platform functions.

## 9. Relationship with other rules

This Standard does not replace the Terms of Use, Deposit and Damage Rules, Payments Rules, Moderation Rules, or Privacy Policy. It helps explain in practical terms how these rules apply in real situations.
