# Naudokis Moderation, Complaints, and DSA Rules

## Moderation process operating principles

The Naudokis moderation process is based on proportionality, explainability, and the ability to appeal a decision. This means:

- notices of illegal Content or illegal items are assessed according to the submitted facts, evidence, and the basis in legislation or Platform rules;
- where possible, the decision explains which Content, Listing, account, payment, or Reservation is affected;
- the User is provided with practical information on how to remedy the violation or submit a complaint;
- automated signals may help detect risk, but significant restrictions are reviewed by a human where required by law, the nature of the situation, or a User complaint;
- frequent manifestly unfounded or malicious notices and complaints may lead to temporary suspension of processing of that reporting person's notices and complaints.

Naudokis may apply different urgency regimes. Manifestly dangerous, illegal, fraud, threat, minor safety, stolen-item, or product safety cases may be handled more urgently and may lead to temporary restrictions before a final decision.

## 17. Moderation, DSA, AI, and account restrictions

**In brief:** Naudokis has the right to restrict Content, Listings, accounts, Reservations, payments, or payouts where this is needed because of legislation, safety, fraud, misleading Consumers, product safety, or Platform rules. Where mandatory, we provide reasons for the decision and contesting options.

### Moderation, illegal Content, and DSA contact point

Naudokis has the right to investigate, moderate, restrict, suspend, terminate, or remove accounts, Listings, messages, Content, reviews, recommendations, Reservations, or other activity if we reasonably believe that law, these Terms, App rules, safety standards, consumer protection requirements are violated, or a risk arises to Users, the Platform, or third parties.

Separately from Content moderation, Naudokis may also temporarily hold or adjust payments, payouts, Deposits, refunds, or Reservations because of payment-provider rules, payment Disputes, fraud prevention, taxes, compliance, Disputes concerning item condition, or other lawful operational reasons. Such actions may be related to Platform rules, but they are not necessarily Digital Services Act Content moderation decisions.

Measures may include a warning, Content removal, message blocking, temporary or indefinite Listing suspension, Reservation cancellation, refund, Deposit allocation, payout hold, review hiding, marking a recommendation as potentially deceptive, account restriction, account deletion (termination of use of the Platform), or notice to competent authorities.

#### Notice and action mechanism

Any person may report potentially illegal Content, prohibited items, unsafe Listings, abuse, fake reviews, or other violations through the reporting functions in the App or by email at **info@naudokis.lt**. The notice should contain sufficient information to make a decision, including:

- a sufficiently substantiated explanation of why the Content is considered illegal or in breach of Platform rules;
- a clear electronic location of the relevant Content where available (for example, Listing link, Listing ID, message identifier, account name, screenshot, or another clear reference);
- the reporting person's full name and email address, except where the law permits reporting without providing this information;
- confirmation that the notice is submitted in good faith and that the information stated in it is accurate and complete to the reporting person's knowledge.

After receiving a notice, Naudokis confirms receipt if the reporting person provided a contact address, and reviews the notice in a timely, diligent, non-arbitrary, and objective manner. We inform the reporting person and, where appropriate, the affected User of the decision.

Naudokis has the right to reject manifestly unfounded or abusively repeated notices and complaints where permitted by applicable law.

If prior warning is required under the Digital Services Act before temporary suspension of notice or complaint handling, Naudokis provides such warning. The frequency, content, impact, and intentions of abuse are assessed. Such decisions are made carefully and do not restrict the right to submit a substantiated notice or complaint.

Where Naudokis receives a notice from a trusted flagger recognized by a competent authority under the Digital Services Act, such notice, where applicable, is handled as a priority and without undue delay, while maintaining objective and non-discriminatory assessment.

If Naudokis becomes aware or has reasonable grounds to believe that an item or service offered through the Platform is illegal, unsafe, recalled from the market, counterfeit, or otherwise in breach of applicable requirements, Naudokis has the right to take proportionate measures.

Such measures may include removing or restricting the Listing, suspending related Reservations, holding payments or payouts, requesting evidence from the Lessor, or notifying competent authorities.

Where required by applicable law, Naudokis may also inform affected Renters or Consumers about an illegal or unsafe item, the known Trader or Lessor, and possible remedies.

Naudokis has the right to apply risk-based checks of Listings, categories, Traders, higher-risk items, and repeated violations. These checks do not remove the Lessor's responsibility for the item, Listing, safety, lawfulness, and accuracy of information.

#### Statements of reasons and notices to the affected User

When we make a decision to remove Content, restrict its visibility, suspend or terminate account functions, suspend a Listing, hide a review, or apply another significant measure because of illegality of Content, a breach of these Terms, or a breach of Platform rules, we provide the affected User with an explanation of the decision to the extent required and permitted by applicable law.

The explanation, where applicable and where safe to disclose, states:

- the type and scope of the measure;
- whether the measure is temporary or final, and its duration or review conditions;
- the main factual circumstances relied on;
- the breached provision of the Terms, Platform rules, or law;
- whether automated or AI tools were used to make or prepare the decision;
- what actions the User may take to remedy the violation, if possible;
- how to submit a complaint, request human review, or use other remedies;

Naudokis has the right to limit disclosure of details where necessary to protect fraud prevention, security, law enforcement, privacy, trade secrets, payment providers, other Users, or third-party rights.

For submission of decisions and statements of reasons to the European Commission DSA transparency database and publication of transparency reports, see Appendix 23.5 of these Rules.

Where payments, payouts, Deposits, refunds, or Reservations are held because of payment-provider rules, payment Disputes, taxes, compliance, fraud prevention, item-condition Disputes, or similar reasons, Naudokis provides an explanation through support, payment, or Dispute processes. The explanation is provided to the extent permitted by law, payment-provider rules, security needs, and the rights of other persons.

Such an explanation usually states the category of the hold reason, what is needed for review, whether the decision is temporary, and how to provide additional information.

#### Internal complaint-handling system

Where applicable under the Digital Services Act or internal Naudokis rules, Users may complain free of charge and electronically about Naudokis decisions concerning removal or visibility restriction of Content, Listing or account suspension, restriction, or termination of use of the Platform, hiding of a review, and decisions not to take action based on a submitted notice.

This right is granted for at least 6 months from the relevant decision and for other periods provided by applicable law. Complaints may be submitted through the App's support functions or by email at **info@naudokis.lt**.

Payment, payout, Deposit, refund, and Reservation Disputes are handled according to the payment, Deposit, cancellation, and Dispute sections of these Terms, as well as according to payment-provider procedures where applicable.

Where under the Digital Services Act the right to appeal a decision applies not only to the affected User but also to the person who submitted the notice, that person may also use the electronic complaint process stated in this section.

Complaints are reviewed by competent staff and not exclusively by automated tools. If the initial decision was made automatically or an automated tool had a significant influence on the decision, human review is ensured during the complaint where required by legislation or by the significance of the decision to the User. We review complaints in a timely, diligent, non-arbitrary, and objective manner. If the complaint is justified, we reverse the initial decision without undue delay and, where possible, restore the affected Content or function.

#### Suspected criminal offences

If we become aware of information giving reasonable grounds to suspect that a criminal offence involving a threat to the life or safety of persons has occurred, is occurring, or may occur, we will inform the law-enforcement or judicial authorities of the relevant Member States where required by applicable law.

#### Out-of-court dispute settlement under the Digital Services Act

Where such right applies under the Digital Services Act, if you disagree with a Naudokis decision concerning Content moderation or another decision falling within the scope of the Digital Services Act, you have the right to apply to an out-of-court dispute settlement body certified in a European Union Member State and having the relevant competence.

Consumer Disputes concerning rental services, payments, refunds, Trader obligations, or other Consumer rights matters are not the same as Digital Services Act Content moderation Disputes. Such Consumer Disputes are handled under Sections 18 and 21 of these Terms and applicable Consumer rights procedures.

#### Contact point

Under Articles 11-12 of the Digital Services Act, the Naudokis electronic contact point for Users, Member State authorities, the European Commission, and the European Board for Digital Services is the email address info@naudokis.lt. Communication languages: Lithuanian and English. Naudokis will seek to respond to competent-authority and User enquiries within a reasonable time.

### Artificial intelligence and automated tools

Naudokis has the right to use automated or semi-automated tools for Platform operation, security, moderation, support, fraud prevention, preparation of Listing descriptions, User verification, Reservation eligibility checks, payment and payout readiness, review of reviews or Content, and other functions.

During the launch period, Naudokis uses AI and automated tools in a limited manner:

- proposed Listing description drafts;
- detecting message Content signals, for example potentially prohibited contact details or attempts to conclude a transaction outside the Platform;
- rule-based prohibited-item filtering;
- rule-based review flagging;
- first-line support chats.

In support chats, the AI support assistant may respond directly to the User's support request. The User may select the support request category.

Handover to a human (escalation) is rule-based, not AI-based. The chat is transferred to a Naudokis employee when the chat threshold is reached or certain key signals are detected, for example signals relating to payments, Disputes, damage, fraud, law, or an express request to communicate with a human.

Significant account, payment, Deposit, or payout-related decisions are not based solely on an AI support chat.

Where the AI Act, consumer law, the Digital Services Act, or other applicable law requires clear information to the User, Naudokis provides the relevant notice in the App, support chat, Listing creation process, or another appropriate interface point. This applies where the User interacts with an AI system or where Content was generated or materially altered by an AI tool.

During the launch period, Naudokis does not use AI for emotion recognition, biometric categorization, social scoring, or identification of sensitive characteristics.

An AI-generated draft Listing description is presented to the User as an auxiliary suggestion. The User must review it, correct it, supplement it, reject it, or replace it with the User's own text, and is responsible for the accuracy, completeness, lawfulness, and compliance with these Terms of the final Listing. Where applicable law requires disclosure of AI use, Naudokis provides the relevant clear notice in the App or another appropriate interface.

During the launch period, Naudokis does not use a general User fraud score, fully automated account suspensions, or fully automated account deletions (terminations of use of the Platform).

Automated payout holds may be applied where clear operational reasons exist. Such reasons may include an open Dispute, compliance or tax hold, disabled Lessor payouts, a self-billing mandate that has not been accepted, late and escalated return, condition mismatch, or unconfirmed handover.

The User may contact Naudokis regarding such hold through the support functions and request human review where the decision has significant consequences for the User or where required by legislation.

AI or automated-tool results cannot be the sole basis for a final significant adverse decision concerning account deletion, final non-payment of a payout, final non-refund of a Deposit, or a similar legally significant effect, unless this is expressly permitted by applicable law and mandatory safeguards are provided.

### Account suspension and termination

You may stop using the Platform at any time, but you must perform or properly resolve active Reservations, payments, refunds, Disputes, tax, compliance, and other legal obligations.

Naudokis has the right to suspend, restrict, or terminate your access to the Platform or specific functions if you breach these Terms, create risk, fail verification, payment, or compliance checks, misuse the Platform, violate the law, or create a safety, fraud, payment, tax, reputational, or legal risk.

Before suspension or termination, Naudokis usually provides notice and an opportunity to remedy the breach where this is reasonably possible and mandatory by law. Prior notice may not apply or may be limited where it is necessary to immediately protect other Users, the Platform, the payment system, evidence, security, a law-enforcement investigation, a tax or compliance process, or where required by payment-provider rules or mandatory law.

After account suspension or termination, the provisions that by their nature must survive continue to apply, including provisions concerning payments, Disputes, Deposits, taxes, Content licence, privacy, liability, damages, retention, remedies, and applicable law.

### 23.5 Content moderation, complaints, and DSA decisions appendix

Naudokis has the right to take measures concerning Content, Listings, accounts, Reservations, payments, Deposits, payouts, reviews, messages, or functions if there is a justified risk that these Terms, legislation, payment-provider rules, product safety requirements, Consumer rights, privacy, intellectual property rights, fraud prevention, or Platform security rules are breached.

Where Naudokis makes a decision to restrict Content visibility, remove a Listing, suspend an account, restrict a function, hold a payout, temporarily hold a Deposit, or otherwise affect a User's use of the Platform because of alleged illegality or breach of the Terms, the User is provided with clear and specific information.

Information is provided to the extent required by law and to the extent this does not undermine security, fraud prevention, other Users, trade secrets, or law-enforcement investigations.

A typical statement of reasons may include:

1. the type of decision and the time it takes effect;
2. the affected Listing, Content, Reservation, payment, Deposit, payout, or account;
3. the main facts or signals relied on;
4. the applied clause of the Terms, Platform rule, or legal basis;
5. whether automated tools or AI assistance were used to make or prepare the decision;
6. the duration of the decision or the conditions that must be met;
7. the impact on payments, Deposits, payouts, or Reservations;
8. how to submit a complaint or appeal;
9. the time limit for submitting a complaint, if such time limit applies;
10. information about the right to contact a competent authority, dispute settlement body, or court where such right applies.

The User may contest a moderation or restriction decision through the App support channel or by email at **info@naudokis.lt**, providing the decision identifier, explanation, and evidence.

Complaints are assessed by Naudokis employees or authorized persons. AI or automated signals may help select, classify, or prepare information, but significant decisions concerning an account, Listing, payment, Deposit, or payout should usually be reviewed by a human where required by legislation or where the User submits a substantiated complaint.

Where Naudokis is required under the Digital Services Act to provide a decision and a statement of reasons to the European Commission DSA transparency database, Naudokis does so without undue delay and ensures that the submitted information does not contain Personal Data to the extent required by applicable law.

Where Naudokis is required under the Digital Services Act to publish transparency reports and the micro-enterprise or small-enterprise exemption does not apply to it, Naudokis publishes such reports within the scope and time limits set by legislation. If Naudokis voluntarily publishes additional information about moderation, safety, or Platform operation, such information does not replace mandatory reports where they apply.
