# Naudokis Deposit, Damage, Loss, and Dispute Rules

Effective date: June 1, 2026
Version: 2026-06-01
Language: English
Last updated: June 1, 2026

These Rules form part of the Naudokis Terms of Use and must be read together with the main document. If a special term is provided in the App for a specific Reservation, Listing, User role, or Platform function, it applies to the extent it does not conflict with mandatory law.

## Evidence and decision standard

In the Naudokis Dispute process, Naudokis assesses not only a party's statement, but the totality of evidence. The most important evidence is evidence submitted on time, linked to the specific Reservation, and allowing comparison of the item's condition before handover and after return.

| Situation | Main evidence | Possible outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Item returned without issues | Return confirmation, photographs, no claim | Deposit refund is initiated according to the payment process |
| Late return | Agreed return time, actual return time, messages | Additional rental fee, substantiated losses, or use of the Deposit |
| Missing accessory | Accessories list recorded at handover, return photographs, messages | Cost of replacing or restoring the accessory, if justified |
| Ordinary wear | Use duration, nature of the item, before-and-after photographs | Usually not treated as compensable damage caused by the Renter |
| New damage | Before-and-after photographs, repair estimate, incident explanation | Partial or full use of the Deposit, additional claim if justified |
| Loss or theft | Absence of return, parties' messages, police or insurance information, evidence of item value | Use of the Deposit and additional claim up to the substantiated item value |
| Item did not match the Listing | Renter's notice at receipt, photographs, Listing text | Partial or full refund, Reservation adjustment, or Lessor liability |
| Illegal or dangerous item | Notice, photographs, authority or manufacturer information | Listing removal, Reservation cancellation, refund, account restriction |

Naudokis may make an interim decision if it is necessary to quickly stop a payout, protect a Deposit, suspend a Listing, or protect other Users, and make the final decision after additional evidence.

## Practical Dispute timeframe scheme

- The Renter must report an obvious non-conformity or defect as soon as possible after receipt.
- The Lessor must submit a claim for damage, loss, or delay as soon as possible after item return - usually within 24 hours (see the section "Deposit claim and evidence"). If the deadline is missed, the Deposit refund to the Renter is usually initiated.
- The other party must have a reasonable opportunity to respond, submit counter-evidence, and provide explanations.
- A Deposit or payout may be temporarily held while the Dispute is being reasonably reviewed.
- If the evidence does not substantiate the claim, the Deposit refund is initiated according to the payment process.

The later evidence is submitted, the harder it is to link it to the specific Reservation. Late submission of evidence may affect the outcome of the Dispute.

## 11. Deposits

**In brief:** The Deposit is usually authorized together with the payment and captured after Lessor acceptance and successful payment completion, unless the App states otherwise. It is refunded after the rental if there is no substantiated claim. The Deposit is not insurance, a guarantee, escrow, or a liability limit.

| Situation | What usually happens to the Deposit? |
| --- | --- |
| Rental completed without issues | The Deposit refund is initiated according to payment-provider, bank, and card-network timeframes. |
| The Lessor submits a substantiated claim on time | The Deposit may be temporarily held while evidence is assessed. |
| The evidence substantiates only part of the claim | Part of the Deposit may be refunded, and another part used for the substantiated claim. |
| The item is lost, seriously damaged, or returned incomplete | The Deposit may be used to cover a substantiated claim, but this does not in itself limit possible additional liability under law. |
| The Dispute is not resolved internally by the Platform | The parties may contact the payment provider, authorities, or court. |

**Example:** if you rent a projector and return it on time, functioning, and with all accessories, the Deposit refund is usually initiated. If the remote control is missing or the projector is damaged, the Lessor may submit a claim with evidence, and the Renter may respond. The example is provided only for explanation and does not limit these Terms or mandatory law.

### Main Deposit rules

A Deposit may apply to a Reservation. The Deposit amount, refundable nature, payment method, and main terms are shown in the App before the Renter confirms payment.

**Important: during the launch period, the Deposit is usually authorized together with the Reservation payment in the Stripe payment process and captured only after Lessor acceptance and successful payment completion.**

If a different Stripe configuration applies in a specific case, this must be clearly stated on the payment screen before payment confirmation. Where the Deposit is captured, the Renter must understand that this amount is actually deducted from the payment instrument and may later be refunded, partially refunded, temporarily held, or used according to these Terms.

The Deposit is not:

- insurance, insurance coverage, or a Naudokis promise to compensate damage;
- a guarantee that the Lessor will always receive compensation;
- a limit on the Renter's liability if substantiated losses exceed the Deposit amount;
- a separate banking deposit, client funds, trust, or escrow account;
- an interest-bearing account or invested funds.

Deposits are processed through the Platform payment process, Naudokis accounting, and Stripe or other payment-participant systems. Naudokis is not a bank, credit institution, electronic money institution, insurer, guarantor, or independent escrow agent. The fact that Naudokis administers a Deposit does not in itself mean that Naudokis becomes a party to the Rental Transaction or assumes the contractual duties of the Lessor or Renter concerning the item.

### When the Deposit is refunded, held, or used

After the rental, the Deposit may be:

- refunded to the Renter in full;
- partially refunded;
- temporarily held while a Dispute, payment Dispute, security, fraud, tax, compliance, or technical issue is reviewed;
- used in full or in part to cover a substantiated Lessor claim, if the claim relates to damage arising after item handover, loss, theft, missing accessories, late return, improper use, or other substantiated liability.

If no substantiated claim is submitted within the claim submission period indicated in the App and there is no open Dispute, Naudokis initiates a refund of the full Deposit to the Renter. The period is calculated from confirmation of item return, automatic marking of item return on the Platform, or rental completion.

Unless the App states otherwise, such claim period is usually 24 hours from confirmed item return or rental completion. The time for funds to become visible in banks or payment networks may differ and does not depend on Naudokis.

If a Reservation is cancelled before handover of the item, the Deposit is refunded in full. The cancellation policy applies only to the rental price and other clearly stated non-refundable fees, but not to the Deposit. The Deposit is refunded in full even where the Renter's cancellation under the Listing cancellation policy is subject to a lower rental-price refund.

### Deposit claim and evidence

If the Lessor states that the item was returned damaged, lost, incomplete, late, or otherwise in breach of the terms, the Lessor must submit a notice and evidence within the period indicated in the App or support communication.

Unless the App states otherwise, a Deposit claim must usually be submitted within 24 hours from confirmed item return or rental completion, before the next rental of that item and, to the extent possible, before the Lessor payout.

A shorter period may apply where needed because of another confirmed Reservation, payment-provider time limits, theft, fraud, or another urgent risk. A longer period may apply where the issue could not reasonably have been identified within the ordinary period or where required by mandatory law. If the Deposit has already been refunded by that time, the claim is handled as an additional claim, and the already refunded Deposit is not charged again.

The Lessor's claim must be supported by appropriate evidence, for example:

- handover and return photographs;
- timestamps, condition notes, and accessories list;
- messages;
- repair estimate or repair invoice;
- substantiation of replacement value;
- police or other authority documents, where applicable;
- explanation why the claim exceeds ordinary wear typical of rental.

Ordinary wear that could reasonably be expected according to the nature of the item, rental duration, and ordinary use usually cannot be a basis for not refunding the Deposit.

Naudokis has the right to request additional evidence, give the Renter an opportunity to respond, assess both parties' information, and make an internal Platform decision concerning Deposit refund, partial refund, temporary hold, or use.

Unless the App or support communication states otherwise, the Renter is usually given up to 7 days to respond to a Deposit claim. A shorter period may apply where necessary because of payment-provider time limits, fraud, theft, security, legal requirements, or another urgent risk.

A Naudokis decision concerning the Deposit must be based on available evidence, applicable rules, the principle of proportionality, and mandatory law. To the extent permitted by law, security needs, payment-provider rules, and the rights of other persons, the decision states the Dispute outcome, main reasons, amount used or refunded, and further review or contesting options.

Additional amounts for damage, loss, late return, or other losses may be charged only where they are clearly stated in the App, separately confirmed, substantiated by a Dispute decision, permitted by payment-provider rules, and not contrary to applicable law.

## 13. Handover, return, damage, and Disputes

**In brief:** Clear evidence of handover, return, photographs, accessories, and condition helps avoid Disputes or resolve them according to facts.

**This section is important for both parties.** For the Lessor, it explains what evidence is needed for a claim. For the Renter, it explains how to respond and protect the Renter's position.

### Handover, return, and condition evidence

Users must comply with the pickup, delivery, handover, and return instructions indicated in the App.

Naudokis has the right to request or, for certain categories, require that the parties confirm item handover or return and provide evidence. This may include photographs, notes, condition information, accessories list, and timestamps. For high-value, risky, or additional-safety-requirement categories, such evidence may be a mandatory part of the Reservation process.

The Lessor must hand over the item in the condition stated in the Listing. The Renter must inspect the item at the time of receipt and immediately report observed defects, non-conformities, or safety issues. The Renter must return the item on time, in the agreed manner, with all accessories, and in the agreed condition, except for ordinary wear typical of rental.

If evidence is not submitted, is submitted late, is incomplete, or is contradictory, this may affect payouts, refunds, Deposit allocation, the outcome of the Dispute, reviews, and account restrictions. This does not mean that failure to submit evidence alone automatically determines the outcome of the Dispute; Naudokis assesses all available information, proportionality, and mandatory law.

### Late return, damage, loss, and improper use

The Renter may be liable for late return, damage to the item, loss, theft, missing parts or accessories, unusual wear, unlawful or unauthorized use, cleaning, recovery, repair or replacement costs, and other substantiated losses arising from improper use or breach of these Terms.

During the launch period, Naudokis does not apply an automatic late-return fee formula unless the specific Reservation flow or App clearly states otherwise before the Renter confirms payment.

If such formula is not clearly stated, late return does not in itself create an automatic fixed fee. However, the Renter may be liable for losses substantiated by evidence where such liability is permitted by applicable law.

A no-show is a case where a party does not arrive at the agreed handover or return place within the reasonable time stated in the App or parties' communication. It may also be a case where a party unjustifiably fails to allow handover or return of the item or does not provide sufficient evidence that non-performance was caused by circumstances beyond the party's control.

In a no-show case, Naudokis has the right to help the parties reschedule handover or return, cancel the Reservation, initiate a refund or hold, open a Dispute, hold a payout, apply account measures, or refer the matter to competent authorities where needed.

If the item is not returned, is hidden, sold, pledged, transferred to a third party, left in a non-agreed place, or there are reasonable signs that it has been lost, stolen, or misappropriated, the Lessor and the Renter must immediately cooperate with Naudokis. They must submit available evidence and, where reasonably necessary, contact the police or another competent authority.

In the event of total loss or theft, a substantiated claim may include the actual replacement value. When assessing it, the item's age, condition, market value, wear, included components, submitted purchase, repair or value evidence, and mandatory law are taken into account. An unjustifiably stated replacement value may not be used as an automatic claim amount.

The Lessor may be liable for inaccurate Listings, undisclosed defects, unsafe items, failure to hand over the item, failure to perform a confirmed Reservation, misleading information, or improper handover.

Additional amounts for late return, damage, or other losses may be charged only where they are clearly stated in the App, separately confirmed, substantiated by a Dispute decision, permitted by payment-provider rules, and not contrary to applicable law.

### Opening a Dispute and time limits

Either transaction party may report an issue or open a Dispute where permitted by the App or support processes. Disputes may relate to item condition, damage, loss, theft, non-delivery, late return, missing accessories, payments, refunds, Deposit, or other Reservation issues.

Unless the App or support communication states otherwise:

- the Lessor's claim for damage, loss, missing accessories, late return, or use of the Deposit must usually be submitted within 24 hours from confirmed return or rental completion;
- the Renter's notice of item non-handover, material non-conformity, unsafety, or misleading information must be submitted as soon as possible after noticing the issue;
- the party against whom the claim is submitted is usually given up to 7 days to respond and submit counter-evidence;
- Naudokis has the right to request additional information and set an additional deadline, taking into account the complexity of the Dispute, payment-provider time limits, security risk, and mandatory law.

Deadlines may be extended or shortened if reasonably necessary because of the nature of the Dispute, payment-provider time limits, legal requirements, security risk, theft, fraud, possible abuse, another confirmed Reservation, or another urgent circumstance.

### Evidence and right to counter-evidence

Users must provide truthful, complete, and current information. Evidence may include photographs, notes, messages, delivery or handover records, return records, payment information, invoices, repair estimates, substantiation of replacement value, authority documents, and other information related to the Dispute.

Naudokis usually gives the other party an opportunity to access the substance of the claim and submit a response where this is necessary for fair handling of the Dispute. Naudokis has the right to limit disclosure of certain information where necessary to protect privacy, security, fraud prevention, law enforcement, trade secrets, payment-provider rules, or third-party rights.

If a party does not submit a response within the set deadline, Naudokis has the right to decide the Dispute based on the available evidence. Failure to respond is not in itself considered an admission, but may reduce the ability to refute evidence submitted by the other party.

### Possible Dispute outcomes

In the Dispute process, Naudokis may:

- temporarily hold a payout, Deposit, or refund;
- request additional information;
- give the other party an opportunity to respond;
- assess submitted evidence;
- reject unfounded, late, bad-faith, or insufficiently substantiated claims;
- refund funds to the Renter;
- pay funds to the Lessor;
- split amounts between the parties;
- use, refund, or partially refund the Deposit;
- apply account, Listing, review, or other Platform measures;
- refer the matter to the payment provider, law enforcement, consumer rights, data protection, tax, or another competent authority where needed;
- close the Dispute when sufficient information is available or a party does not respond within the set deadline.

Naudokis decisions concerning Disputes and Deposits are internal Platform decisions. They are made according to these Terms, the rules stated in the App, submitted evidence, payment-provider rules, and applicable law.

The decision, to the extent permitted by law, security needs, payment-provider rules, and the rights of other persons, states:

- the Dispute outcome;
- the main factual circumstances;
- the applicable rule or legal basis;
- the amount used or refunded;
- the evidence relied on;
- possible further measures.

If you disagree with an internal Naudokis decision, you may request review and submit additional arguments or evidence through the App support functions or by email at **info@naudokis.lt**.

Internal Naudokis Dispute review does not remove or restrict the right to use mandatory Consumer rights, payment-provider procedures, contact the State Consumer Rights Protection Authority, the State Data Protection Inspectorate, another competent authority, or a court.

### 23.2 Deposit, damage, loss, and Dispute rules

The Deposit is a refundable amount clearly stated in the App before payment. During the launch period, the Deposit is usually **authorized together with the Reservation payment and captured after Lessor acceptance and successful payment completion**, unless the specific payment interface clearly states otherwise.

The Deposit is not:

- insurance, insurance coverage, or a Naudokis promise to compensate damage;
- a guarantee that the Lessor will always receive compensation;
- a limit on the Renter's liability if substantiated losses exceed the Deposit amount;
- a separate banking deposit, client funds, trust, or escrow account;
- an interest-bearing account or invested funds.

The Deposit may be fully refunded to the Renter, partially refunded, temporarily held, or, to the extent permitted by these Terms, the submitted evidence, payment-provider rules, and applicable law, used to cover substantiated Lessor claims concerning damage, loss, delay, missing accessories, unusual wear, or other Reservation breaches.

The Lessor's claim concerning the Deposit must be substantiated by evidence. Evidence may include handover and return photographs, an accessories list, description of defects, correspondence in the App, repair estimate, substantiation of replacement value, delay evidence, third-party documents, or other objectively assessable information. A general statement alone that the item was damaged is usually not a sufficient basis for holding the Deposit.

If the App does not state other deadlines for the specific Reservation, the Lessor must submit the claim and initial evidence within 24 hours from confirmed item return or, if return is not confirmed, from the scheduled return time.

If the Lessor does not submit a claim within the indicated period, the Deposit refund is usually initiated according to payment-provider, bank, and payment-network timeframes. An exception may apply where there is a justified risk of fraud, security, law enforcement, payment Dispute, technical error, or breach of mandatory legal requirements.

If the claim is submitted on time, the Renter must be given the opportunity to access the substance of the claim and, to the extent this does not violate security, privacy, or investigation integrity, the main evidence.

The Renter may submit explanations, counter-evidence, and the Renter's proposed solution within the period indicated in the App. Naudokis has the right to extend or shorten periods if reasonably necessary because of weekends, holidays, the nature of evidence, payment-provider time limits, security, fraud prevention, or mandatory law.

The internal Naudokis decision concerning the Deposit or payment adjustment is made according to the available evidence, these Terms, the terms of the specific Reservation, the item description, ordinary wear typical of rental, the principle of proportionality, payment-provider rules, and applicable law.

The decision may be:

- full Deposit refund;
- partial refund;
- use of part or all of the Deposit for a substantiated claim;
- temporary hold;
- temporary payout hold;
- payment adjustment;
- recommendation that the parties resolve the Dispute before a competent authority or court.

The internal Naudokis process does not replace the right of the Lessor or Renter to contact the payment provider, consumer rights protection authority, data protection authority, law enforcement, another competent authority, or a court. A Naudokis decision concerning the Deposit is not an arbitral award and does not in itself remove the parties' right to dispute the claim under the procedure established by legislation.
