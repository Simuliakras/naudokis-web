## 15. Business Users, transparency, and search

**In brief:** If you use the Platform for business or professional purposes, additional identity, representation, Consumer information, tax, VAT, DAC7, VIES, Stripe, and payout requirements may apply to you.

**This section is most important for Business Users, Business Lessors, Business Renters, and Traders.**

These Terms and the information provided in the App explain the Platform rules and operating logic. They are not individual tax, accounting, VAT, DAC7, consumer-law, or other legal advice. Business Users are responsible for assessing their own obligations and, where necessary, consulting qualified advisers.

### Business Users, Business Lessors, Business Renters, and Traders

Business Users may act as Business Lessors, Business Renters, or both, if this is permitted by the Platform functions, the terms of the specific transaction, the payment provider's requirements, and applicable law. Where a Business User rents an item for business or professional purposes, it acts as a Business Renter, and such a transaction may be considered a B2B transaction.

Business Users must provide accurate and up-to-date business profile information, including legal or trading name, registration number, country, registered address, billing contacts, VAT status, public business name, and other information required by law, Platform rules, invoicing, tax, or payment-provider requirements.

Before listing items, accepting paid Reservations, or receiving payouts, a Lessor must correctly select the account, Trader, and tax status. If the Lessor acts for business or professional purposes, systematically offers items, seeks commercial benefit, acts through a legal person or individual business activity, or is considered a Trader under consumer law, the Lessor may not present itself as a private individual.

Naudokis has the right to request additional information or evidence in order to assess whether a User must be considered a Business User, Trader, or Business Renter. If the information is not provided, is inaccurate, or is contradictory, Naudokis has the right to restrict publication of Listings, acceptance of Reservations, payouts, or other functions until the information is clarified.

### Trader traceability and self-declaration

Where a User offers items to Consumers through the Platform as a Trader, Naudokis, before allowing such Trader to conclude distance rental contracts with Consumers through the Platform, requires the Trader to provide, and verifies to the extent reasonably possible using available means, the Trader information required by law. This may include:

- the Trader's full name or legal name;
- address, telephone number, and email address;
- information from an identity document, electronic identification, or another identity verification measure, where the Trader is a natural person and where such information is required by law or by Platform checks;
- registration number, register name, or equivalent information, where applicable;
- VAT identification number or VAT status, where applicable;
- payment account or Stripe Connect account information to the extent required for payments, payouts, and compliance requirements;
- information about the right of representation, where the Trader is represented by another person;
- a self-declaration that the Trader will offer through the Platform only lawful, safe, accurately described items or services that comply with applicable legislation.

Naudokis has the right to use public registers, VIES, Stripe, identity verification providers, documents, or other reasonable sources to verify this information.

If the Trader does not provide the required information, does not correct inaccurate information, or there is a reasonable suspicion that the information is inaccurate, Naudokis suspends or prevents use of those Trader functions for which such information is necessary. This may include Listings, Reservations, payouts, or other functions. The restriction applies until the information is provided, clarified, or verified.

To the extent required by applicable law and necessary for Consumers to make an informed decision, the Trader's public business information must be shown in the App. This may include the public business name, legal name, registration number, country, VAT status indicator, contact channel, information about Consumer rights, and the fact that the Lessor acts as a Trader.

Private or excessive information is not displayed if its public display is not necessary or permitted.

If Naudokis is considered an online marketplace under the Digital Services Act or other applicable law, a Trader may not be allowed to conclude distance contracts with Consumers until the traceability information required by law has been collected and reasonably assessed. The Trader's self-declaration regarding the compliance of the offered items or services must also be collected.

Naudokis has the right to re-check Trader information periodically or on a risk basis, request that it be updated, and restrict functions if the information becomes outdated, inaccurate, or cannot be verified.

### Business Users' duties to Consumers and Naudokis

Business Users and Traders are themselves responsible for the consumer protection, product safety, tax, VAT, invoicing, accounting, permit, insurance, advertising, transparency, and other legal obligations applicable to them.

This includes the duty to provide correct pre-contractual information, not use misleading information, comply with applicable withdrawal or return rights, provide invoices or other documents where required, and ensure that the offered items are safe and lawfully rented.

Naudokis has the right to provide technical tools, templates, status labels, invoicing or self-billing functions, VIES checks, tax data fields, and support processes, but this does not release the Business User or Trader from the obligations applicable to it.

The Business User must immediately update business, VAT, representation, billing, contact, bank, Stripe, DAC7, or other compliance information if it changes. Naudokis has the right to hold payouts, suspend Reservations, or restrict functions if this information is inaccurate, incomplete, outdated, or does not meet payment, tax, or legal requirements.

### Ranking, search, and visibility

Search, recommendation, category, map, or other visibility results may depend on various parameters, for example:

- match with the search query, category, location, dates, and availability;
- price, delivery, or pickup options;
- Listing quality and completeness, photographs, and Lessor response speed;
- verification status, ratings, reviews, cancellation or Dispute history;
- safety and compliance signals;
- User-selected filters, recency, administrator review, or other Platform trust and operation parameters.

Naudokis has the right not to display Listings, or to display them in a lower position, where they are inactive, unavailable, under review, restricted, suspended, related to an open Dispute, non-compliant, or raise a safety, payment, compliance, or legal risk.

If Naudokis introduces paid advertising, sponsored ranking, or another paid visibility increase, this will be clearly indicated in the App or in the relevant Platform notices.

### Business User restrictions, suspensions, and complaints

If Naudokis restricts, suspends, or terminates a Business User's Listings, Reservations, payouts, account functions, or account, Naudokis provides reasons to the extent permitted by law, security needs, payment-provider rules, and the rights of other persons.

Such a decision may be made because of a breach of the Terms, inaccurate information, compliance, payments, taxes, product safety, consumer protection, prohibited items, fraud, or another justified risk.

The explanation may state:

- the type of restriction, its duration, or review conditions;
- the main factual circumstances;
- the provision of the Terms or law that was violated;
- whether automated tools were used;
- what actions are needed to remove the restriction;
- how to submit a complaint or additional information.

If a restriction, suspension, or termination applies to a Business User under the Platform-to-Business transparency regulation or other applicable platform business-user law, Naudokis provides a statement of reasons based on objective criteria within the period and scope required by law.

Business Users may contact Naudokis free of charge through the App's support functions or by email at **info@naudokis.lt** to request review of a restriction. Naudokis handles complaints proportionately, within a reasonable time limit, and, where a decision has significant consequences, ensures human review unless this is prohibited or restricted by law, security requirements, or payment-provider requirements.

Naudokis has the right to change the terms applicable to Business Users and Platform functions under the section of these Terms concerning changes. Where mandatory under legislation applicable to business users, Naudokis provides prior notice within the applicable period, unless the change is necessary due to legislation, security, fraud, technical operation, payment provider, product safety, or another urgent reason.

### 23.4 Business Users, Traders, and B2B terms appendix

A Business User must provide accurate and up-to-date information reasonably required for Platform operation, Consumer information, payments, payouts, taxes, DAC7, VIES, DSA Trader traceability duties, P2B transparency, or other legal obligations. This includes business, representation, registration, VAT, billing, tax, Stripe, invoicing, and other required information.

Naudokis has the right to request declarations or data required to determine whether a specific User, activity, category, or transaction falls within the scope of DAC7, VAT, VIES, invoicing, DSA Trader traceability, or other rules. A data request to assess compliance requirements does not in itself mean that the User or all of the User's transactions will be reportable to tax authorities; reporting is made only to the extent mandatory under applicable law.

A Business User offering items to Consumers must independently assess whether it is considered a Trader under consumer law.

Where a User offers items to Consumers through the Platform as a Trader, Naudokis requires such User to confirm Trader status and provide the Trader information required by law.

Payment or Stripe Connect data, identity or representation confirmations, and confirmation that the offered items and services comply with applicable product safety, consumer protection, licensing, labelling, instruction, guarantee, tax, and other requirements may also be required.

Trader or Business User information that must by law be provided to Consumers or other transaction parties is displayed or made available in the profile, Listing, Reservation flow, invoice, contract summary, or support or Dispute process.

This may include the public business name, legal name, registration number, country, registered office or business address, contact details, VAT status, Consumer rights information, and other mandatory data. Naudokis has the right not to display broader information publicly if it is needed only for verification, payments, taxes, or compliance purposes.

Naudokis suspends or prevents use of those functions for which mandatory information is required. This may include functions for publishing Listings, accepting Reservations, payouts, or other functions.

Such a restriction may apply if the Business User does not provide mandatory information, provides inaccurate information, does not confirm Trader status, does not provide the self-declaration required by law, does not meet Stripe or tax requirements, creates a risk of misleading Consumers, or breaches these Terms.

Reasons for such a decision are provided to the extent required by law and to the extent this does not undermine fraud, security, or law-enforcement investigations.

If applicable law requires, Naudokis provides the main parameters of ranking, sorting, or search results that may determine Listing visibility. These may include category, location, dates, availability, price, User-selected filters, Listing quality, response speed, reliability indicators, reviews, safety or moderation status, payment or payout readiness, advertising or administrative measures if such measures are enabled.

Naudokis is not required to disclose details that would make it possible to manipulate the system, bypass safeguards, or harm Platform operation.
