### 6.3 Business and tax data

**This section is most important for Business Users, Traders, and their representatives.**

If you use a business account, act as a Trader, Business Lessor, or Business Renter, or represent a Business User, we process business, representation, payment, and tax information, including:

- the legal business name, public business name, registration number, country, and registered office address;
- billing email address, billing telephone number, VAT status, and VAT identification number, where applicable;
- confirmation of business representation, business verification status, and Stripe business profile status;
- self-billing consent;
- the tax status of the seller, buyer, Lessor, Renter, or Trader;
- tax identifiers, DAC7 declarations, and seller, buyer, or Business User reports;
- invoice data and compliance status.

If you provide an EU VAT identification number for B2B settlement, invoicing, or tax compliance purposes, Naudokis has the right to perform a **VAT number format check and a VIES check through the European Commission system**.

Such a check is carried out only where it is required for an actual Platform function or legal obligation. The check result (valid or invalid), the European Commission VIES request identifier, and the time of the check may be retained for Reservation, invoice, and tax audit purposes.

Naudokis also has the right to periodically re-check the validity of a provided VAT identification number through the VIES system. If repeated checks do not confirm the validity of the VAT identification number for several consecutive months, Naudokis may automatically stop applying the taxation and invoicing treatment corresponding to VAT payer status to the User until valid data is confirmed. The User is informed of such change. The User may at any time provide a valid VAT identification number so that the status can be checked and restored under the usual procedure, and may also contact **info@naudokis.lt** or use the App's support functions for human review or to contest such decision.

DAC7 or similar platform-reporting data is collected and used only to the extent necessary to determine or perform applicable tax obligations. Because the scope of DAC7 depends on the activity category and legislation, Naudokis has the right to request information to determine status, but reports to tax authorities only those Users, transactions, or activities that must be reported under applicable law.

### 22.6 Appendix F: Business Users, Traders, DAC7, and tax privacy appendix

Business User and Trader data is processed not only for Platform functions, but also for consumer information, Trader traceability, payments, payouts, KYC, VAT, VIES, DAC7, invoicing, accounting, tax, Dispute, and legal-obligation purposes.

This may include the legal name, public business name, registration number, registered office address, representative details, VAT status, VAT identification number, tax identifiers, bank or Stripe information, transaction amounts, payouts, invoice data, self-billing consent, DAC7 declarations, and compliance status.

Where required by law, part of the Trader's information may be provided to Renters, Consumers, other transaction parties, payment providers, tax authorities, or other competent authorities. Naudokis has the right to restrict a Business User's functions or payouts if mandatory data has not been provided, is inaccurate, cannot be verified, or gives rise to legal, tax, payment, or consumer-misleading risk.

DAC7 and similar tax data is retained and provided to the extent necessary to comply with legal requirements, prepare, correct, substantiate, and defend reports, and defend the rights of Naudokis or Users.

Where such data also falls within the DSA Trader traceability rules, the shorter DSA retention period applies only to data that is no longer needed on another lawful basis. Tax, accounting, or Dispute data may be retained for longer.

When determining DAC7 status, Naudokis has the right to process signals relating to category, transaction type, Lessor status, remuneration, and other information in order to assess whether the activity falls within the scope of mandatory reporting. This assessment is not tax advice to the User and does not mean that all Platform item-rental transactions are automatically reported to tax authorities.
