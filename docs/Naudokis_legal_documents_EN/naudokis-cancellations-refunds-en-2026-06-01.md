## Practical cancellation and no-show matrix

This matrix explains the general logic. The cancellation term shown in the App before payment applies to the specific Reservation, provided it does not conflict with mandatory consumer protection.

| Situation | General principle |
| --- | --- |
| The Renter cancels before Lessor acceptance | Payment is usually not finally charged; cancellation of the authorization or a refund is initiated according to the Stripe process |
| The Renter cancels after confirmation | The cancellation term shown in the App applies, including possible service fees, non-refundable parts, or refund |
| The Lessor cancels without a justified reason | A refund is usually initiated for the Renter, and consequences may apply to the Lessor's account, rating, or visibility |
| A party does not attend handover | Communication, the agreed time, waiting period, evidence, and the other party's reasonable losses are assessed |
| The item is not handed over | A refund to the Renter, Listing or account restriction, and impact on the Lessor's rating may be initiated |
| The item is not returned on time | Late fees, Deposit hold, additional losses, and account restrictions may apply |
| The item is unusable at the time of receipt | If the item is unusable at handover through no fault of the Renter, the Renter receives a full or proportionate refund of the amount paid; the Renter must report this without delay and provide evidence |
| Force majeure or safety circumstances | Naudokis may proportionately adjust the refund, cancellation, payout, or rating impact by assessing evidence and mandatory law |

## Platform cancellation right

Naudokis may cancel or suspend a Reservation where this is necessary to manage Platform risk. Such grounds may include payment failure, an identity or age-verification issue, a prohibited item, a safety risk, possible fraud, legal requirements, a DSA or illegal Content notice, a product safety risk, Stripe restrictions, or another justified ground.

In such a case, Naudokis applies a proportionate decision: refund, payout or Deposit hold, Listing restriction, request for additional information, or restriction of account functions, depending on the situation.

## 12. Cancellations, refunds, and the right of withdrawal

**In brief:** Cancellation rules depend on the transaction type, rental date, start of the service, and mandatory law. The Listing cancellation policy never removes mandatory Consumer rights.

| Transaction type | Brief explanation | Consumer rights |
| --- | --- | --- |
| P2P | A private individual rents to another private individual | B2C consumer rights usually do not apply, but general civil, safety, payment, and Platform rules apply. |
| B2C | A Business User or Trader rents to a Consumer | Mandatory Consumer rights apply, including pre-contractual information and, where applicable, the right to withdraw from a distance contract. |
| B2B | A Business User rents to a Business User | Consumer rights usually do not apply; commercial, tax, VAT, invoicing, and contractual rules apply. |

**Example:** if an item is rented by a private individual, that person is usually not a Trader. If an item is rented by a company or a professionally acting person, mandatory Consumer rights may apply to the Renter acting as a Consumer. The example is provided only for explanation and does not limit these Terms or mandatory law.

The cancellation and refund outcome depends on the specific situation: the Reservation and payment status, the timing of cancellation, the start of rental, delivery or dispatch status, the cancellation policy stated in the Listing, Dispute status, fault of the parties, rules stated in the App, and mandatory law.

Before the Lessor's confirmation, a Reservation request may be rejected, expire, or be cancelled. In such a case, a started payment process is usually not completed or is cancelled. If funds have already been charged because of the payment provider or technical process, they are refunded according to the rules of Stripe, payment networks, and applicable law.

If the Lessor cancels an already paid confirmed Reservation before the item is handed over, the Renter is usually entitled to a refund of the entire amount paid. A different outcome is possible only where reasonably permitted by applicable law, Platform rules, or the specific circumstances.

In such a case, Naudokis may apply measures to the Lessor, including a warning, a monetary penalty (which may be set off against the Lessor's Platform credit or deducted from future payouts), Listing pause, account restrictions, or other measures provided in these Terms. The amount of the applicable penalty, or the principle by which it is calculated, is shown to the Lessor in the App or in the Platform rules.

If the Renter cancels a confirmed Reservation, the cancellation policy stated in the Listing applies, **but it never limits mandatory Consumer rights that apply by law to the specific transaction**. Cancellation policies may include, for example:

- **Flexible** - full refund if cancelled at least 24 hours before the start of the rental; if cancelled later, the rental price is non-refundable, except where mandatory law provides otherwise.
- **Moderate** - full refund if cancelled at least 5 calendar days before the start of the rental; 50% refund if cancelled 1-4 calendar days before the start of the rental; if cancelled later, the rental price is non-refundable, except where mandatory law provides otherwise.
- **Strict** - 50% refund if cancelled at least 14 calendar days before the start of the rental; if cancelled later, the rental price is non-refundable, except where mandatory law provides otherwise.

If the Listing does not state otherwise, the Moderate cancellation policy applies.

In all cancellation cases, the refund percentages described above apply only to the rental price. Clearly stated upfront non-refundable fees are not refunded, except where mandatory law requires otherwise. The Deposit, if it was charged, is refunded to the Renter in full in the event of cancellation before the item is handed over.

Standard refunds may not apply after delivery has started, after the rental has started, less than 24 hours before the start of the rental, or where non-refundable costs have already been incurred, unless mandatory law, Lessor fault, a Naudokis decision, or Platform rules require otherwise.

### Determining the transaction type

Before the Renter confirms payment, the App must clearly state information about the parties' status to the extent permitted by Platform functions and to the extent available. This includes whether the Lessor acts as a private individual, Trader, or Business User, and whether the Renter acts as a Consumer or Business User.

The transaction type determines which Consumer rights, cancellation rules, invoicing, VAT, B2B, or other obligations may apply.

General principle:

- **P2P transactions** - where the Lessor is a private individual and the Renter also acts outside business or professional purposes, Consumer rights applicable to contracts with Traders usually do not apply, but mandatory civil, safety, fraud, payment, and other law applies.
- **B2C transactions** - where the Lessor acts as a Trader or Business User and the Renter acts as a Consumer, mandatory consumer protection rules apply, including mandatory pre-contractual information and, where applicable, the right to withdraw from a distance contract.
- **B2B transactions** - where both parties act for business or professional purposes, Consumer rights usually do not apply, but commercial, tax, VAT, invoicing, platform business-user, and other rules may apply.

If a User incorrectly states their status, conceals Trader status, or attempts to bypass consumer protection, tax, payment, or platform rules, Naudokis has the right to clarify the status, require additional information, suspend Listings or payouts, cancel Reservations, or take other proportionate measures.

### 14-day right of withdrawal

Where a Rental Transaction is concluded between a Renter acting as a Consumer and a Lessor acting as a Trader, the Renter has the right to withdraw from a distance contract within 14 days, except for exceptions provided by law.

This right usually does not apply to P2P transactions between two private natural persons where the Lessor does not act as a Trader. It also usually does not apply to B2B transactions.

Legislation may provide exceptions to or limitations on the right of withdrawal. The right of withdrawal does not apply only in cases set by law, for example to certain categories of services (accommodation, vehicle rental, catering, or leisure services) where the contract provides for a specific date or period, where the service has been fully performed with the Consumer's express consent and acknowledgement that the right of withdrawal will be lost, or where another specific exception applies under mandatory law.

If the 14-day right of withdrawal applies to a specific Rental Transaction, information about the method, time limit, and consequences of exercising it must be provided in the App or during the Reservation flow. Where required by legislation, a model withdrawal form or equivalent clear tool is also provided.

If an exception to or limitation on the right of withdrawal applies to a specific Rental Transaction, the App or Reservation flow must clearly state what exception or limitation applies, to the extent reasonably possible according to the Platform functions and the information provided by the Trader.

Where mandatory law requires an electronic withdrawal function, withdrawal button, model withdrawal form, or another special online tool, Naudokis provides it in the App, on the website, or in the support process. This is done to the extent such function applies to Naudokis as the Platform operator or to a process administered on behalf of the Trader.

If such function has not yet been activated, the Consumer may exercise the right of withdrawal by a clear statement through a support channel or by email, unless mandatory law requires a more specific method.

If the Consumer requests that the rental service or preparation for its provision begin during the withdrawal period, the App may request separate clear consent to start performance. Confirmation may also be requested that the Consumer understands the possible consequences for the right of withdrawal.

Such consequences may include an obligation to pay a proportionate amount for the part of the service provided before withdrawal, or loss of the right of withdrawal, where this is clearly permitted by mandatory law. A fixed record of the consent and information may be retained for Dispute and legal-claim purposes.

Where the 14-day right of withdrawal applies, the Renter acting as a Consumer may exercise it by a clear and unambiguous statement. The statement may be submitted to the Trader (Lessor) indicated in the Reservation and/or to Naudokis as the Platform operator through the App's support functions or by email at **info@naudokis.lt**.

The model withdrawal form is not mandatory if the Consumer provides any other unambiguous statement of the decision to withdraw from the contract.

If the statement is submitted to Naudokis where the party to the Rental Transaction is a Trader, Naudokis forwards the statement to the relevant Trader or makes it possible for the Trader to access it through Platform processes, to the extent necessary to administer the right of withdrawal.

After the applicable right of withdrawal has been exercised, amounts paid are refunded without undue delay and no later than within 14 calendar days from the date the notice of withdrawal from the contract is received. The refund is usually made using the same payment instrument used for the payment, unless applicable law or clearly stated Trader terms permit otherwise.

If the Consumer expressly requested that performance of the service start during the withdrawal period, the Consumer may be obliged to pay for the part of the service actually provided before withdrawal, as provided by applicable law.

These cancellation rules do not limit mandatory Consumer rights that may apply to the relationship between the Renter and the Trader, including rights relating to misleading information, improperly provided service, an unsafe item, an item not handed over, incorrect indication of Trader status, or other cases provided by legislation.

Refunds may be delayed or temporarily held if a Dispute is open, a payout has already been made, payment-provider review is required, there is a payment Dispute, fraud is suspected, additional information is required, or a review by the Naudokis support team or administrator is ongoing.

This provision does not apply to the extent mandatory Consumer law sets a specific refund deadline or prohibits delaying a refund for the stated reason. In such cases, the mandatory deadline more favorable to the Consumer applies.

### 23.3 Cancellation, refund, and right of withdrawal rules

Cancellation, refund, and right of withdrawal rules depend on the transaction type, the parties' status, rental dates, nature of the item, moment when the service starts, cancellation policy stated in the App, and mandatory law. The cancellation policy stated in the App applies only to the extent it does not conflict with mandatory Consumer rights.

| Situation | Main rule |
| --- | --- |
| A private individual rents from a private individual | The 14-day right of withdrawal applicable to transactions with Traders usually does not apply to P2P transactions; however, the cancellation and refund rules stated in these Terms and in the App remain in force. |
| A Consumer rents from a Business User or Trader | Mandatory Consumer rights apply, including information obligations and the right of withdrawal, unless a lawful exception applies. |
| The rental relates to specific dates or a specific period | The right of withdrawal does not apply only in cases set by law, for example vehicle rental or leisure services with a specific date or period. For other rental services, the 14-day right of withdrawal remains. |
| The Consumer expressly requests that the service begin during the withdrawal period | If mandatory law permits, the Consumer's right of withdrawal may be limited or the Consumer may have to pay a proportionate part for the service already provided, but only if mandatory information was provided and the necessary confirmation was obtained. |
| B2B transaction | The Consumer right of withdrawal usually does not apply, unless mandatory law or specific terms provide otherwise. |

If a Consumer has the right to withdraw from the contract, the Consumer may submit a clear statement through the App support channel, by email at **info@naudokis.lt**, or through the contract withdrawal function in the App where such function is available.

From the date on which an electronic contract withdrawal function is mandatory under applicable law, and where the right to withdraw from the contract applies to the specific Rental Transaction, Naudokis provides through the App or another supported electronic interface a clear contract withdrawal function or another tool complying with mandatory legal requirements.

Such function does not replace the right to submit another clear and unambiguous statement where mandatory law permits this.

Naudokis has the right to request information necessary to identify the Reservation. However, a lawful withdrawal may not be rejected solely because the Consumer did not use a specific form, if the Consumer's statement clearly shows the wish to withdraw from the contract.

The procedure for forwarding the statement to the Trader is described above in this document.

Refunds are made through the payment provider according to the deadlines of the payment method, banks, card schemes, and payment provider.

If a Reservation is cancelled, rejected, automatically expires, payment fails, or the contract is lawfully withdrawn from, the refunds stated in the App and these Terms apply. Only permitted Platform fees, amounts for services proportionately provided, payment Dispute holds, or other lawful adjustments may be deducted from them.

If mandatory Consumer law sets a specific refund deadline or prohibits delaying a refund for a specific reason, the mandatory deadline more favorable to the Consumer applies.
