### 6.2 Identity, email, and telephone verification data

**In brief:** Identity and contact verification is used for trust, security, payments, payouts, fraud prevention, and compliance requirements. Naudokis usually does not retain a permanent archive of raw documents unless this is needed for a clearly stated purpose.

Naudokis requires verification for certain actions important for trust, security, payments, or compliance requirements, including publication of Listings, reservation of items, payout setup, Dispute resolution, and fraud prevention.

We may process only information necessary for the specific verification, security, or compliance purpose, including:

- verification status, session identifiers, and timestamps;
- rejection reasons, retry information, and expiry dates;
- provider results and selected identity attributes;
- duplication or fraud signals;
- document expiry, legal name, date of birth, and other information provided by the verification provider.

Identity verification is provided by **Didit**. Didit may process identity document images, document verification data, face or liveness check data, technical verification signals, and related information under its contract with Naudokis and according to the legal requirements applicable to Didit.

Where Didit acts on Naudokis instructions, Didit acts as a Processor. Where Didit must independently perform legal, security, or regulatory obligations, it may act as an independent Controller under its own privacy notices and contracts.

Face or liveness check data is used only for identity document, liveness, age, fraud-prevention, and verification purposes. Face and liveness checks for these purposes are performed by the identity verification provider Didit; face images and biometric templates are not permanently retained in Naudokis systems - only verification results and related technical signals are retained. Naudokis does not use this data for general biometric categorization, emotion recognition, identification of sensitive characteristics, marketing profiling, or a general User reputation score.

Biometric data used to identify a person is special-category Personal Data (GDPR Article 9). It is processed only where a condition provided in GDPR Article 9(2) applies (for example, your explicit consent, which you give when starting identity verification), after an assessment of necessity and proportionality, and with appropriate safeguards.

Naudokis generally retains verification status, audit information, verification-validity information, and selected verification results, not a permanent archive of raw identity documents. Raw identity document images are retained in Naudokis systems only where and only to the extent necessary for a specific verification, Stripe Connect, Dispute, security, fraud, payment, tax, or legal-claim purpose.

For fraud-prevention purposes, Naudokis has the right to retain a cryptographically protected technical security tag. It helps determine whether the same identity document or personal identification code is used to create multiple accounts.

This tag is not the raw document number or personal identification code and must be created and retained so that Naudokis does not use it to reconstruct the original document number or personal identification code. The technical security tag is used only to detect multiple accounts, abuse, and fraud, is not used for marketing, general User profiling, or a general reputation score, and is periodically reviewed according to retention necessity.

During the launch period, this technical security tag is retained as part of the identity verification record while the account is active and to the extent necessary to manage risks relating to multiple accounts, bypassing an account block or other restrictions, payment fraud, or security.

Upon final account deletion, this record is usually deleted together with the identity verification record, unless the specific record must be temporarily retained on a limited basis because of an active investigation, Dispute, payment Dispute, legal obligation, or legal defence.

If a Lessor completes the Stripe Connect registration or onboarding process, Naudokis has the right to transfer to Stripe verified identity attributes received from Didit and, where necessary, identity document images. Such data may also be transferred where required for payment, anti-money laundering, sanctions, tax, or payout compliance purposes.

Transferred attributes may include, for example, first name, last name, date of birth, address, or document information.

Stripe processes this data as an independent Controller for its regulated payment, KYC, sanctions, fraud-prevention, tax, and Dispute-handling purposes under the Stripe privacy notice. For certain technical operations, Stripe may also act as a Naudokis service provider or Processor.

Identity status verified in the Naudokis system may be considered valid for up to **730 days** (24 months), unless the identity document expires earlier, a security or fraud risk arises, legal requirements change, the payment provider requires re-check, or re-verification is necessary because of Platform functions.

## 11. Payments, identity verification, and regulated Processing

Payment services on the Platform are provided by Stripe and other participants in the payment ecosystem. Naudokis itself does not retain all payment card numbers and does not provide regulated payment services as a bank, credit institution, or electronic money institution.

Stripe may process payment methods, card or payment-instrument data, payment authorization and capture information, refunds, payment Disputes, Stripe Connect accounts, payouts, sanctions, anti-money laundering, fraud-prevention, tax, and financial-compliance data.

For these regulated and independent purposes, Stripe usually acts as an independent Controller under its own privacy notice. Where Stripe performs limited technical operations according to Naudokis instructions, Stripe may act as a Processor or service provider under contract.

Identity verification is provided by Didit. Didit may process identity document, liveness, document-authenticity, age, name, date of birth, document-validity, technical, and security signals.

For the identity verification service performed according to Naudokis configuration and instructions, Didit acts as a Naudokis Processor. In the specific cases where Didit independently performs legal, security, abuse-prevention, service-integrity, or regulatory obligations under its privacy notice and applicable law, Didit may act as an independent Controller for those separate purposes.

Where a Lessor, Business User, or other User must use Stripe Connect, receive payouts, or pass payment compliance checks, Naudokis has the right to transfer to Stripe Didit-verified identity attributes or document images only to the extent necessary for Stripe Connect, KYC, sanctions, payments, payouts, Disputes, taxes, or performance of legal obligations.

Naudokis has the right to retain payment and payout identifiers, Stripe customer, payment, connected-account, transfer, refund, and payment Dispute references, reconciliation records, payment statuses, Deposit statuses, and related audit records. These records are needed for Platform operation, refunds, Deposits, Disputes, accounting, taxes, DAC7, fraud prevention, legal defence, and User support.

Deposit data is processed as Reservation, payment, Dispute, and accounting data.

During the launch period, the Deposit is usually recorded as included in the Stripe payment authorization together with the Reservation payment. It is recorded as charged after the Lessor's acceptance and successful payment completion, unless the specific payment interface clearly states otherwise.

Naudokis processes Deposit statuses, hold reasons, refund or allocation records, evidence links, and audit data to the extent necessary to administer the Deposit and resolve Disputes.

### 22.4 Appendix D: identity verification, document technical security tag, and fraud prevention

Identity verification is used for trust, age, fraud prevention, payments, payouts, Disputes, Trader traceability, and legal-obligation purposes.

Naudokis generally retains verification status, audit metadata, selected verification attributes, retry or rejection reasons, and validity information. However, Naudokis does not seek to accumulate a permanent archive of raw identity documents unless this is necessary for a specific lawful purpose.

For fraud prevention, Naudokis has the right to retain a cryptographically protected technical security tag of the document number and/or personal identification code. It helps detect whether the same document or personal identification code is used for multiple accounts.

This tag is not the raw document number or personal identification code, is not used for marketing, and is not intended for public profiling. It is used only for security, fraud prevention, prevention of multiple accounts, prevention of bypassing account blocks or other restrictions, and compliance purposes.

During the launch period, the technical security tag is retained as part of the identity verification record while the account is active. Upon final account deletion, it is usually deleted together with that record, unless limited retention is necessary because of an active fraud investigation, Dispute, payment Dispute, legal obligation, or legal defence.

Where a Lessor or Business User uses Stripe Connect or other regulated payment functions, Naudokis has the right to transfer identity, business, document, or verification attributes to Stripe to the extent necessary for KYC, AML, sanctions, payments, payouts, payment Disputes, or legal-claim purposes. Stripe may process this data as an independent Controller under its privacy notice and regulated obligations.
