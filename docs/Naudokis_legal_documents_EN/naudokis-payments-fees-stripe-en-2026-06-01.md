## 10. Payments, fees, and Stripe

## Payment and payout status matrix

This matrix explains how Naudokis practically assesses payment, Deposit, and payout statuses. The status of the specific Reservation shown in the App must be interpreted together with this table and Stripe processes.

| Status | What it means for the Renter | What it means for the Lessor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Payment initiated | The payment process has started but is not yet finally confirmed | The Reservation is not yet finally paid |
| Payment authorized | The payment instrument has been checked; the amount (including the Deposit, where applicable) is reserved on the Renter's payment instrument but has not yet been captured | Acceptance may lead to final capture if Stripe confirms the payment |
| Payment captured | The Reservation amount and, where applicable, the Deposit are captured according to the payment screen | The payout may still be pending until the Reservation is properly completed and there are no Disputes |
| Payment failed | The Reservation may be unconfirmed or cancelled | The Lessor does not have to perform an unpaid Reservation unless the App clearly shows otherwise |
| Deposit held for review | Part or all of the Deposit amount is temporarily held because of a substantiated claim | The Lessor must submit evidence through the established process |
| Refund initiated | The refund has been transferred to the payment process; the actual return of funds to the account depends on the bank and card networks | The Lessor payout may be adjusted according to the refund |
| Payout pending | This usually does not change the Renter's fulfilled payment obligation if the payment was not disputed or reversed | The payout has not yet been transferred because of Reservation completion, Stripe, compliance, Dispute, or Platform review |
| Payout transferred | The Renter's payment is completed unless a payment Dispute later arises | The amount has been transferred according to the Stripe or Platform process, but may be adjusted if a payment Dispute, fraud, or legal basis later arises |
| Payment Dispute or chargeback | The Renter's bank or card network is reviewing a payment Dispute | Payouts may be held, reversed, or set off according to Stripe and applicable rules |

## Payment transparency standard

Before the Renter confirms payment, the App must clearly display the information applicable to the specific Reservation:

- rental price;
- Renter service fee;
- Lessor fee or payout deduction;
- delivery or pickup fee;
- Deposit;
- applicable taxes;
- cancellation and refund rule;
- total amount payable;
- whether the amount is authorized, captured immediately, or captured after Lessor acceptance.

If the information shown on the payment screen differs from general help-center or policy wording, the clear information shown on the payment screen before confirmation is most important for the specific Reservation, provided it does not conflict with mandatory provisions of law.

**In brief:** Payments are made through Stripe. Before payment, the App must clearly show the price, fees, Deposit, refund and cancellation terms, and other essential terms.

### Payments, fees, and payouts

Payments on the Platform are processed through Stripe and, where applicable, Stripe Connect infrastructure.

During the launch period, Naudokis uses a platform payment authorization, later capture, and later payout model:

- the Renter's payment is usually first authorized in the Stripe payment process;
- the payment is captured after Lessor acceptance, if Stripe successfully completes the capture;
- the amount due to the Lessor may be transferred through Stripe Connect (transfers functionality);
- before payout, applicable fees, refunds, Deposit holds, payment Disputes, compliance-check holds, or other permitted adjustments may be deducted.

Naudokis itself does not provide regulated payment services and is not a bank, credit institution, or electronic money institution. Regulated payment services are provided by Stripe and other payment participants.

Naudokis acts as the Platform operator. By using Stripe and Stripe Connect, Naudokis has the right to collect or administer the Renter's payment, apply Platform fees, hold or delay payouts, make refunds, administer Deposits, and transfer the amount due to the Lessor according to these Terms, the rules provided in the App, Stripe rules, and applicable law.

The fact that a payment, Deposit, refund, Dispute, or payout is administered through the Platform does not in itself mean that Naudokis becomes the owner, seller, lessor, or party to the Rental Transaction for the rented item.

By using paid Platform functions, you agree to comply with the applicable Stripe terms, payment processes, and verification requirements. Stripe, payment networks, banks, or other payment participants may apply their own checks, restrictions, refund, payment Dispute, and payout rules.

**Payment administration arrangement.** To the extent permitted by applicable law, the Renter's payment made through the Platform's Stripe payment process and successfully confirmed by Stripe is considered performance of the Renter's payment obligation to the Lessor for the relevant Reservation.

This applies only to the extent the payment is not later rejected, refunded, disputed, found unlawful, fraudulent, or otherwise reversed under Stripe, card-network, bank, or applicable-law rules.

The Lessor agrees that settlement for paid Platform Reservations is carried out through the payment process indicated on the Platform. The Lessor may not require the Renter to pay directly in addition for the same Reservation, unless the payment did not occur, was reversed, or this is permitted by these Terms and mandatory provisions of law.

Naudokis administers payment instructions, payout accounting, refunds, Deposits, holds, and adjustments as the Platform operator according to these Terms, the rules disclosed in the App, payment-provider rules, and mandatory provisions of law.

Such administration is not a banking deposit, client funds, trust, or escrow service. It does not give the User the right to require a payout earlier than all payout conditions set in these Rules are met: the Reservation is properly completed, applicable refund, Deposit, and payment Dispute issues are resolved, compliance and tax checks are completed, and there are no legal obstacles.

Naudokis decisions concerning a refund, Deposit hold, payout hold, payment adjustment, or payment Dispute administration are internal Platform administration decisions. They do not remove Users' right to dispute a payment through payment-provider procedures, contact competent authorities, or apply to a court where such right applies.

Payments on the Platform are accepted and executed in euros (EUR) through Stripe payment infrastructure. The Platform is intended for Reservations carried out in Lithuania. If a different Stripe configuration, payment method, authorization logic, direct or indirect capture process, payout structure, or additional consent applies to a specific function, this is clearly shown in the App before payment confirmation or before using such function.

The Renter must pay the amounts stated in the App before payment confirmation. They may include rental price, delivery fee, Renter service fee, Deposit, applicable taxes, and other clearly disclosed payments.

If a Deposit applies to a specific Reservation, during the launch period it is usually included in the same Stripe authorization as the Reservation payment. The Deposit is captured only after Lessor acceptance, if Stripe successfully completes the capture, unless the App clearly states otherwise.

Naudokis has the right to apply a Lessor Platform fee, Renter service fee, minimum transaction fee, Deposit processing fee, delivery fee, or other fees clearly stated in the App. Fees may be applied to the Renter as payable amounts or to the Lessor as payout deductions, depending on the nature of the specific fee.

Before the relevant User confirms payment, accepts a Reservation, or performs another fee-related action, the App displays the specific fee amounts, percentages, possible discounts, credits, taxes payable to the state, Deposits, payer, basis of application, and impact on the total amount payable or the Lessor payout.

The Deposit processing fee, if applicable, is a Naudokis fee related to administration of Deposit payment, accounting, refund, holding, or allocation.

Unless the App states otherwise, this fee applies to the Lessor and is deducted from the payout payable to the Lessor. It is not added separately to the amount payable by the Renter and is not part of the Deposit.

This fee usually applies only when the rental starts or is completed or when a Deposit-related process actually takes place. It does not apply where the Reservation is cancelled before handover of the item and Deposit-related administration has not actually occurred.

Fees may differ according to User type, function, transaction, category, campaign, individual agreement, or another lawful basis. This includes whether the transaction is a Consumer, Business Renter, Business Lessor, or B2B transaction.

Naudokis has the right to change fee amounts, but changed fees apply only to future Reservations and do not apply to already confirmed Reservations, unless required by law, payment-provider rules, or the parties expressly agree otherwise.

Where a User acts as a Consumer, mandatory information about the total price, Deposit, refund and cancellation rules, Lessor status, applicable fees, and payment consequences is provided in the App before payment confirmation to the extent required by mandatory consumer protection legislation.

Where a User acts as a Business Renter, the App may provide additional billing, invoice, VAT, or other information needed for business accounting.

Lessors receive payouts only after the Reservation is properly completed, the payment is finally credited (settlement through Stripe is completed), the applicable payout waiting period passes, refunds or Deposits are processed, and there are no active payment, Dispute, compliance, tax, moderation, or account restrictions.

Payout timeframes depend on the Lessor's Stripe Connect connected account settings, banks, payment networks, compliance checks, Disputes, payment Disputes, technical processes, or Naudokis review. Therefore, a uniform settlement period cannot be guaranteed.

Naudokis has the right to hold, delay, set off, reverse, adjust, refund, split, transfer, or otherwise process payments and payouts to the extent required for the following purposes:

- administering refunds, Disputes, payment Disputes, or payment errors;
- Deposit allocation;
- fraud prevention;
- compliance with legal requirements, Stripe restrictions, or compliance review;
- enforcement of these Terms.

The Lessor payout is not final until the relevant transfer has been made and no applicable refund, payment Dispute, legal, tax, compliance, or Platform hold basis remains.

If, after payment or a Lessor payout, the payment is disputed, refunded, cancelled, found unlawful, fraudulent, or otherwise not credited, Naudokis has the right, to the extent permitted by applicable law and Stripe rules, to take adjustment actions. This may include suspending future payouts, initiating reversal of a transfer, setting off amounts against future amounts payable to the Lessor, or requiring return of an unjustifiably received payout.

Users must cooperate in providing evidence relating to a payment Dispute, refund, Deposit, item handover, item return, or damage. Naudokis has the right to apply temporary reserves, payment or payout holds, and account restrictions where reasonably required because of a payment Dispute, fraud, negative balance, Stripe restriction, legal obligation, or another payment risk.

### Information before payment confirmation

Before the Renter confirms payment, the payment screen or Reservation summary must clearly provide at least the following information, to the extent applicable to the specific transaction:

- the item being rented;
- rental period (start and end dates);
- Lessor status: private individual, person carrying out individual business activity, company, or other Business User or Trader;
- total amount payable;
- rental price;
- Platform fees;
- delivery or handover fees;
- Deposit amount and whether the Deposit is first authorized and later captured, or whether another payment model clearly stated in the App applies;
- cancellation and refund policy;
- main item usage restrictions;
- whether a possible 14-day right of withdrawal applies or does not apply;
- main consequences of damage and late return;
- whether any insurance, guarantee, or Naudokis compensation protection applies to the specific transaction (usually not applicable - see Sections 11 and 19);
- the party responsible for performing the Rental Transaction (usually the Lessor, not Naudokis, unless these Terms state otherwise);
- a link to these Terms and the Privacy Policy.

A Reservation request is submitted only when the Renter selects a button clearly indicating an obligation to pay (for example, "Confirm and pay"). A finally confirmed and paid Reservation arises only when the Lessor accepts the request and payment is successfully captured or otherwise confirmed; until then, the App may show an interim Lessor acceptance or payment processing status.

If the payment screen states that the Deposit will be authorized and later captured, the User must be provided with clear information about the Deposit process.

Such information must explain that the Deposit will be included in the Stripe authorization together with the Reservation payment and captured only after Lessor acceptance, if Stripe successfully completes the capture.

It must also explain that after the rental the Deposit may be refunded in full, refunded in part, temporarily held, or used to cover a substantiated claim according to these Terms, the submitted evidence, payment-provider rules, and applicable law.

If information required by mandatory Consumer-law provisions or these Terms for a specific transaction is missing before payment, Naudokis has the right to prevent completion of the payment, mark the Reservation as incomplete, or require the Lessor, Renter, or Business User to supplement the information. The payment button, where consumer law applies, must clearly indicate that the action creates an obligation to pay.

Naudokis has the right to retain a fixed record of the Reservation summary, price breakdown, Deposit, cancellation rules, Trader status, right-of-withdrawal information, and other essential terms. It helps substantiate what information was provided at the time of payment or Reservation confirmation.

This fixed record is used for contract performance, Consumer information, Disputes, payments, audit, taxes, and legal requirement purposes.

Payment authorization, successful capture, re-authorization, refund, and Deposit administration possibilities depend on Stripe, card networks, banks, payment instrument, strong customer authentication, fraud prevention, and applicable payment rules.

If a payment authorization expires, the authorization fails, is cancelled, requires additional confirmation, or can no longer be used, Naudokis has the right to request repeat payment or authorization, prevent confirmation of the Reservation, cancel the Reservation, or apply another payment process clearly stated in the App, to the extent permitted by applicable law and Consumer rights.

No additional mandatory fees may be added after payment confirmation unless they were clearly disclosed before payment, arose because of the Renter's later actions, late return, damage, additionally ordered service, legal requirement, payment Dispute, or another ground clearly provided in these Terms.

### Taxes, VAT, DAC7, settlement, and invoices

You are responsible for determining and performing the tax, VAT, business, accounting, trader, purchase, consumer protection, product safety, and other obligations applicable to your rental activity. This applies both to Business Lessors and Business Renters where they participate in B2B or other transactions concluded for business purposes.

Naudokis has the right to collect tax and billing information of the Lessor, Renter, or Business User, classify tax status, request DAC7 declarations, and require self-billing consent.

Where applicable, Naudokis may also issue or help issue receipts, invoices, credit notes, Platform fee invoices, Lessor, Renter, or Business User statements, and tax reports, and provide mandatory information to tax authorities.

**Electronic documents.** Receipts, invoices, credit documents, and other accounting documents related to Reservations are issued in electronic form — in the App and (or) by email. By using the Platform and entering into Rental Transactions, the User consents to receiving these documents in electronic form (including electronic VAT invoices under Article 79 of the Lithuanian VAT Law). The authenticity, integrity, and legibility of the documents are ensured throughout their entire retention period. The User may download their documents in the App at any time.

**DAC7 and platform reporting.** Naudokis is considered a reporting platform operator in cases where activity carried out through the Platform falls within the scope of Council Directive (EU) 2021/514 (DAC7) and the Lithuanian legislation implementing it.

DAC7 application depends on the specific activity category and legislation. The EU DAC7 framework includes, among others, rental of immovable property, personal services, sale of goods, and rental of any means of transport. Therefore, not every item rental carried out through the Platform is automatically considered a DAC7 reportable activity.

Naudokis has the right to collect or request information in order to determine status, but submits mandatory reports only to the extent provided by applicable law.

In such cases, Naudokis has the right to collect, verify, retain, and provide to the State Tax Inspectorate under the Ministry of Finance of the Republic of Lithuania (STI) the information required by legislation about reportable Users and remuneration received through the Platform.

Depending on applicable legislation and the nature of the specific activity, reported data may include first and last name or legal entity name, address, tax identification number (TIN), VAT identification number, date of birth, financial account identifier, amounts of remuneration received, number of transactions, fees collected by the Platform, and other mandatory information.

If mandatory reporting applies to a specific Lessor, Naudokis, to the extent provided by law and Platform functions, provides the ability to review or receive report information relating to that Lessor.

DAC7 and other tax-compliance records are retained for the periods set by legislation. The information provided in this section is not tax advice, and specific DAC7 application must be assessed under legislation in force and the nature of your activity.

**VAT, invoices, and tax reports.** Where applicable, Naudokis has the right to help prepare invoices, credit notes, Platform fee invoices, self-billed invoices, and tax reports according to the functions indicated in the App.

In B2B transactions, the App may request that the Business Lessor and Business Renter provide legal name, registration number, VAT identification number, billing address, billing contacts, and other information required for the invoice or tax-compliance requirements.

The application of VAT, reverse charge, VIES, i.SAF, and other tax obligations depends on the specific transaction, the parties' status, the nature of the service or item, legislation, and STI requirements. Naudokis has the right to process this information and provide data to authorities to the extent required by applicable law.

**Self-billing.** During the launch period, documents for paid Platform Reservations are usually generated through the Naudokis self-billing and settlement process where such function applies to the specific User or transaction. This may include receipts, invoices, credit notes, payout documents, Platform fee documents, and related tax reports.

A Lessor on whose behalf Naudokis issues invoices (self-billing) must confirm the agreement for invoicing on their behalf and provide accurate tax information before receiving payouts. The Lessor must not issue duplicate invoices for the same Platform transaction unless required by law or clearly coordinated with Naudokis through a supported process.

If the Lessor must issue an additional document under law, the Lessor is responsible for ensuring that the document does not conflict with Platform-generated records and does not mislead the Renter.

**Naudokis VAT status.** The Naudokis VAT status is published in the App according to applicable-law requirements. We inform Users in advance of an expected status change where this is material to invoice issuance.

Naudokis does not guarantee that every Lessor can issue a VAT invoice or that costs incurred by a Business Renter will be recognized as deductible expenses, VAT deduction, or another tax benefit. This depends on the Lessor's status, the nature of the transaction, the Business Renter's circumstances, and applicable legislation.

Naudokis has the right to hold payments or payouts or restrict the activity of a Lessor, Renter, or Business User if required tax, invoicing, business, Stripe, or other compliance information is missing, inaccurate, outdated, unverified, or restricted. Compliance reminders and applicable measures are indicated in the App in advance.
