# Naudokis Reviews, Search, and Ranking Rules

Effective date: June 1, 2026
Version: 2026-06-01
Document ID: **NAUDOKIS-REVIEWS-RANKING-EN-2026-06-01**

These Rules explain how Naudokis handles reviews, ratings, Listing visibility, search, and recommendations.

## 1. Main principle

Reviews must reflect an actual rental experience. Listing visibility must be based on signals that are relevant to the User and justified from the perspective of safety and service quality, not on hidden arrangements or manipulation.

## 2. Who may leave a review

A review may be left only by the parties to a Reservation who have duly completed a Reservation through the Platform. Unless stated otherwise in the App, the deadline for submitting reviews is 14 days from completion of the Reservation. Naudokis may prevent a review from being left or remove it if:

- the Reservation was not actually confirmed or completed;
- the review was left by someone other than a party to that Reservation;
- the review is not related to a specific rental experience;
- the review is intended to blackmail, pressure, or retaliate;
- remuneration, a discount, or another incentive was offered for the review;
- the review contains illegal, offensive, discriminatory, misleading Content or Content that unjustifiably discloses Personal Data.

## 3. What is allowed in reviews

It is permitted to honestly describe:

- the condition of the item and its conformity with the Listing;
- the clarity of handover and return;
- communication;
- punctuality;
- compliance with instructions;
- the experience of damage, delay, or a Dispute, if described factually and without abuse.

## 4. What is prohibited in reviews

It is prohibited to publish:

- threats, harassment, hatred, or discrimination;
- unfounded allegations of a criminal offence;
- third-party Personal Data, addresses, document data, or other sensitive information;
- false information intended to harm reputation;
- advertisements, links, invitations to pay, or invitations to communicate outside the Platform;
- Content that violates the Terms or legislation.

## 5. Review moderation

Naudokis may review reviews automatically or manually. Review may begin before publication or after publication, especially if a complaint is received, the review relates to an active Dispute, or it contains potentially illegal Content.

Possible decisions:

- publish the review;
- reject it;
- hide it until the review is completed;
- remove the part that violates the Rules;
- remove the entire review;
- restrict the User's right to leave reviews;
- apply account or Listing restrictions if the review system is used for abuse.

## 6. Complaints about reviews

A User may complain about a review through the App or by email to **info@naudokis.lt**. The complaint must indicate:

- which review you are complaining about;
- why you believe it violates the Rules;
- what evidence you have;
- whether the review is related to an active Dispute concerning damage, a Deposit, cancellation, or payment.

The mere fact that a review is negative is not a basis for removing it. Naudokis removes or restricts reviews where there is a violation of the Rules, illegal Content, abuse, or a need to protect another party's rights.

If your review is hidden or removed, you may appeal that decision through the internal complaint-handling procedure set out in the Naudokis Moderation, Complaints, and DSA Rules, free of charge and electronically.

## 7. Search and visibility signals

The main parameters determining the order of Listings in search, filters, and recommendations, by relative importance, are as follows (most important first):

- the User's search query, location, dates, category, and filters;
- the item's availability during the specific period;
- price, Deposit, delivery or pickup method;
- Listing completeness, photograph quality, and relevance;
- the Lessor's response speed, cancellation history, and completed Reservation metrics;
- reviews, ratings, and trust signals;
- safety, moderation, prohibited-item, or risk restrictions;
- recency, popularity, User interactions, and the Platform's technical operation;
- the Lessor's status as a Business User or Trader, which is always clearly shown to the Consumer and may be used to filter or sort results.

These parameters are most important because they directly reflect the Listing's match with the user's query, item availability, and the quality and safety of the service. We inform Users of material changes to the main parameters in this document or in the App.

Naudokis may adjust visibility if a Listing is incomplete, misleading, risky, violates the Rules, has active Disputes, or restrictions apply to the Lessor's account.

## 8. Paid visibility

If paid Listing highlighting, promotional boosting, or another paid visibility-increase function is enabled on the Platform, this must be clearly marked in the App. Until such a function is enabled, Listing visibility is based only on the Platform's search, quality, availability, safety, and relevance signals.

## 9. Prohibited manipulation

Users are prohibited from:

- creating fake Reservations or reviews;
- using multiple accounts to increase or decrease a rating;
- offering remuneration for a positive review or for not leaving a negative review;
- threatening a negative review in order to obtain unjustified benefit;
- systematically redirecting transactions outside the Platform in order to bypass ratings, payments, or safety processes;
- using automated tools to manipulate Listing visibility.

## 10. Business User transparency

Where a Lessor acts as a Business User or Trader, the Platform clearly displays their business status and other legally required data to the Consumer; additional representation, billing, or Consumer information data may also be displayed. This data may affect Consumer trust and Listing visibility, but must not be used in a misleading manner.
